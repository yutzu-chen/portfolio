Case Study 02

Scaling product operations with automation

I reduced manual coordination work by turning repetitive product operations into automations: translations, A/B test documentation, stale-ticket handling, and internal troubleshooting all became faster and more consistent.

Jira + APIs
Product Ops
Workflow Automation
OpenAI API

Context

At my previous company, product and engineering teams were spending too much time on operational work that should have been routine. Copy translation, experiment documentation, ticket cleanup, and internal support all relied on manual steps.

The problem was not just speed. The bigger issue was consistency. Each workflow was handled slightly differently, which made it harder to keep records clean and keep the team focused on actual product work.

My goal was to reduce operational overhead without making the workflows harder to use.

Understanding the users

I approached the work by finding the core needs of the primary users. The workflows had to answer practical questions quickly: what needed translation, what experiments were live, which tickets had gone stale, and where internal requests were getting stuck.

That framing shaped the requirements. The automations needed to remove manual work, keep information in the right tools, and make the underlying processes easier to follow.

4 projects were delivered

01

Translation automation pipeline

I connected source text to the OpenAI API, generated translations automatically, and pushed the output directly into the translation system. That removed a repetitive back-and-forth, copy-paste process that had previously slowed down multilingual releases.

02

Automated A/B test documentation and announcements

I set up Jira Automation so that when a JIRA ticket moved into development, a structured Confluence documentation page would be created automatically. When the work was done, a Slack announcement would be posted without repeating copy-paste steps.

03

Jira ticket lifecycle automation

I built a lightweight lifecycle flow for stale tickets. Inactive items triggered reminders, and if there was still no response, they could be closed or marked for follow-up. That kept the board cleaner and easier to prioritize.

04

Self-service troubleshooting for internal requests

I wrote self-service docs that guided cross-team members through common fixes when they opened a ticket. The documentation itself was enough to resolve 70% of the specific issues and avoid extra support work.

Impact

80% Time saved on translation work
Cleaner Jira boards with fewer stale tickets
70% Tickets resolved through self-service
  • Removed repetitive manual work from PM and engineering workflows
  • Improved transparency across launch, documentation, and support processes

Lessons learned

Product management is not only about building features for users. It also includes improving the internal systems that help teams move faster and stay organized.

The strongest improvements came from automating repeatable work, keeping workflows maintainable, and designing for the people who actually use the process every week.

Reflection

This project showed me that the best automation work is usually invisible when it is done well. The goal was to remove low-value coordination so the team could spend more time on the product itself.
